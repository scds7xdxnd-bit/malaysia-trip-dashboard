Method & caveats

Luxury-tilted weights · pairwise AHP · CR = 0.004 (consistent)

Core + alpha. Taeyang's ₩2,000,000 core (≈ RM6,600) covers roughly one flagship, not two. The AHP winners — Mandarin Oriental (KLCC, ~RM950) + Shangri-La Tanjung Aru (~RM800) — run ~RM10,400 all-in for two over 7 nights / 8 days together; the ~RM3,800 gap is Taeyang's alpha. Swap MO for Park Hyatt (Merdeka 118, ~RM1,600) only for the ultra-splurge — it is AHP #3 here and lifts the KL bill ~70%.

Island, beach, or city? Re-weighting cut Value to 0.10 and kept Location at 0.16, so the verdict still favours the mainland: Tanjung Aru is the one pick that is genuinely beachfront and 15 min from town and the airport. Gaya Island & Bunga Raya edge it on raw luxury but lose on access (boat-only) and value.

Factcheck flags: Gaya Island & Bunga Raya are boat-access only from Jesselton Point (~15–20 min) — no walk-in or late arrivals. Nexus Karambunai is ~45 min out, effectively stay-put. Park Hyatt KL is in Merdeka 118 (not KLCC) and budget-breaking at ~RM1,600. City hotels (Hyatt Centric, Le Méridien, Hilton) have rooftop pools but no swimmable beach.

Weather & packing. July in KL & Kota Kinabalu: hot (28–33°C), humid, short afternoon downpours; Sabah seas are usually calm — good for island hopping. Pack: linen/cotton, swimwear & reef-safe sunscreen, rain shell, insect repellent, walking shoes, Type-G adapter.
